How global warming is transforming Ghana's environment and way of life

The Science Behind Climate Change

Climate change refers to significant, long-term changes in global temperatures and weather patterns. While climate has changed throughout Earth's history, the current rapid warming is primarily caused by human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas.

Greenhouse Effect

Certain gases in Earth's atmosphere (like carbon dioxide and methane) trap heat from the sun, creating a natural "greenhouse effect." Human activities have intensified this effect, causing global temperatures to rise at unprecedented rates.

How Climate Change Affects Ghana

Ghana faces significant challenges from climate change, impacting agriculture, water resources, coastal zones, and public health. The country's economy, heavily dependent on climate-sensitive sectors like cocoa farming and fishing, is particularly vulnerable.

Northern Ghana experiences prolonged droughts while southern regions face increased flooding. These changes threaten food security, livelihoods, and the achievement of Sustainable Development Goals.
