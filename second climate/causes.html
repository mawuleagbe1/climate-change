Causes & Effects of Climate Change in Ghana

Understanding the drivers and impacts of climate change on Ghana's environment and society

Major Causes of Climate Change in Ghana

Deforestation and Land Use Change

Ghana has one of the highest deforestation rates in Africa, primarily due to:

  • Agricultural expansion (especially cocoa farming)
  • Illegal logging and timber extraction
  • Mining activities (both legal and illegal)
  • Urban expansion and infrastructure development
  • Fuelwood collection (accounts for 70% of energy use in rural areas)

Between 2001 and 2020, Ghana lost 1.41 million hectares of tree cover, equivalent to a 19% decrease since 2000. This deforestation contributes significantly to Ghana's greenhouse gas emissions.

Energy Production and Use

Ghana's energy sector contributes to climate change through:

  • Reliance on fossil fuels for electricity generation (especially during droughts when hydropower is limited)
  • Use of traditional biomass (charcoal and firewood) for cooking in rural areas
  • Increasing vehicle ownership and traffic congestion in urban areas
  • Industrial energy use with limited efficiency measures

The energy sector accounts for about 45% of Ghana's total greenhouse gas emissions.

Agricultural Practices

Agriculture contributes to climate change through:

  • Methane emissions from livestock (especially cattle in northern Ghana)
  • Nitrous oxide emissions from fertilizer use
  • Burning of agricultural residues
  • Conversion of forests to farmland

Agriculture accounts for about 39% of Ghana's total emissions, with enteric fermentation (livestock digestion) being the largest source.

Observed and Projected Effects

Climate change is already affecting Ghana in multiple ways, with more severe impacts projected in coming decades:

Impacts on Agriculture and Food Security

  • Declining cocoa yields due to higher temperatures and changing rainfall patterns (projected 30-40% reduction in suitable cocoa growing areas by 2050)
  • Reduced maize yields (projected 7% decrease by 2050 under moderate climate change scenarios)
  • Shifting agricultural zones as some crops become unviable in current growing areas
  • Increased pest and disease outbreaks affecting crops and livestock
  • More frequent crop failures in northern Ghana due to droughts

Water Resource Challenges

  • Declining water levels in major rivers like the Volta (affecting hydropower generation)
  • More frequent and severe droughts in northern regions
  • Increased flooding in urban areas due to intense rainfall events
  • Deteriorating water quality due to erosion and runoff during heavy rains
  • Conflict over water resources between farmers and herders

Coastal Zone Vulnerabilities

  • Coastal erosion swallowing up to 2 meters of land per year in some areas
  • Saltwater intrusion contaminating freshwater sources and agricultural land
  • Increased flooding of coastal communities during high tides and storms
  • Damage to critical infrastructure like roads and ports
  • Loss of mangrove ecosystems that provide natural protection

Human Health Impacts

  • Increased malaria transmission as mosquitoes expand their range
  • More heat-related illnesses, especially in urban areas
  • Higher incidence of water-borne diseases after floods
  • Malnutrition due to reduced food production
  • Respiratory diseases from dust during droughts and from urban air pollution

Biodiversity Loss

  • Shifting ecosystems as species struggle to adapt to changing conditions
  • Coral bleaching along Ghana's coast due to warmer ocean temperatures
  • Loss of endemic species in forest reserves
  • Disruption of migratory patterns for birds and other animals
  • Reduced fisheries due to ocean acidification and warming

Economic Consequences

Food Security

Staple crop yields could decline by 15-20% by 2050, increasing food prices and malnutrition.

Infrastructure

Coastal infrastructure worth $1.5 billion is at risk from sea level rise and erosion.

Health Costs

Climate-related health costs could reach $300 million annually by 2050.
